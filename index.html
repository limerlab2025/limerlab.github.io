What We Do

Research

Exploring the frontier of intelligent systems and autonomous machines.

🎯

Optimal Control Design

Developing mathematically rigorous control strategies that optimize performance metrics for dynamic systems, including model predictive control and linear-quadratic regulators.

🛡️

Robust Control

Designing controllers that maintain stability and performance under system uncertainties and external disturbances — critical for real-world robotics deployment.

🤖

Robotics

Building and programming autonomous robotic platforms, from motion planning and kinematics to perception-action loops and human-robot interaction.

⚙️

Mechatronics

Integrating mechanical, electrical, and computational systems to create smart machines — bridging the physical and digital worlds through embedded sensing and actuation.

Education

Courses

Courses taught and supported by LIMeR Lab faculty.

EGR 301

Control Systems

Classical and modern control theory. Topics include feedback systems, stability analysis, frequency-domain methods, and PID control design.

EGR 415

Mechatronics

Integration of mechanical design, electronics, sensors, actuators, and microcontrollers. Lab-intensive with hands-on projects.

EGR 450

Robotics Engineering

Kinematics, dynamics, path planning, and robot programming. Students build and program robotic platforms throughout the term.

EGR 480

Advanced Control Design

State-space methods, optimal control (LQR/LQG), robust control (H∞), and model predictive control for advanced engineering applications.

Our Space

Facility

State-of-the-art equipment for cutting-edge robotics and mechatronics research.

🦾

Robotic Platforms

Multiple autonomous and semi-autonomous robotic systems for experimentation in motion planning, manipulation, and multi-agent coordination.

💻

Computing Infrastructure

High-performance workstations and GPU servers for simulation, machine learning, and real-time control algorithm development.

🔧

Electronics & Prototyping

Fully equipped electronics bench with oscilloscopes, signal generators, soldering stations, and 3D printing for rapid prototyping.

📡

Sensing & Actuation

IMUs, LiDAR, cameras, force/torque sensors, and a range of actuators to support physical robot experiments and data collection.
